Key Training Notes
1
Monitor iron levels — especially in high-mileage weeks. Fatigue that doesn't resolve with rest can signal low ferritin. Schedule a blood panel mid-block (August).
2
The 10% rule — never increase weekly mileage by more than 10% week-over-week. Cutback weeks are mandatory, not optional. They're when fitness consolidates.
3
Sleep is training — aim for 8–9 hours during peak block (Weeks 12–14). Growth hormone peaks during deep sleep. Skimping here negates hard workouts.
4
Quality workout structure — always include a proper warmup (1–1.5mi easy) and cooldown (0.5–1mi easy) around tempo, interval, and MP sessions. The adaptation happens in the recovery.
5
Easy really means easy — if you can't hold a full conversation, you're running too fast. Easy runs build your aerobic base without adding fatigue. Most runners run them 45–90 sec/mi too fast.
6
Taper trust — the last 3 weeks will feel strange. Your legs will feel heavy, then bouncy. You'll feel undertrained. You're not. The work is done. Honor the taper.
7
NYC is 3 weeks after Chicago — treat it as a celebration run. Focus completely on Chicago. NYC is the bonus.
NYC Recovery Block — Oct 13 to Nov 1
After Chicago, your body needs 10–14 days before quality running resumes. The 3-week recovery block keeps you moving without digging a hole. Weeks R1–R3 build gently from ~17mi back to race fitness. Listen to your body — if Chicago was a hard effort, extend the easy phase. NYC is about the experience, not the time.
